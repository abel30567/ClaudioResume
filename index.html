Under the supervision of Dr. Richard Bone I joined the Experimental Biophysics Group at Florida International University. I built a heterochromatic flicker photometry optical device that measures an individual's macular pigment optical density that depends on one's diet. Initially it used LEDs, like the design above, and I switched the blue LED with a blue laser to try to achieve monochromaticity and the green LED with an amber one. This was an upgrade because blue and green LEDs have wide intensity curves and this causes an error in calculating one's macular pigment and approximate eye lens. I learned about electronics, worked with optics, chromatography, and photometry equipment.
